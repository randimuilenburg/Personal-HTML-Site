I am seeking representation for RESIN, a standalone young adult urban fantasy novel with sequel potential at 82,000 words, with difficult girls doing wicked things, similarly found in LITTLE MONSTERS by Kara Thomas and THE MERCILESS by Danielle Vega, and a love story like the romance in STARFISH by Akemi Dawn Bowman.

In a former pawn shop, Abbie and her cousin, Laura, craft handmade dolls that manipulate the personalities of the people they know. Abbie can only make dolls while Laura also works the magic behind them.

It starts with Cole, who watches Abbie and makes her feel as if he knows her secrets. Then, it happens to Cassie, the best friend of the girl Laura repeatedly bullies. After her, it’s Justin, the boy who struggles to tell Abbie how he feels, who meets her eyes in the halls and makes her wish for something more too.

Laura expects Abbie to sneer at his affections, so Abbie keeps her own feelings a secret. A doll can’t change anyone unless it’s finished, and she doesn’t plan on finishing Justin’s. As Laura pushes Abbie to work faster, their friendship fractures. For the first time, Abbie steps out from her cousin's shadow to decide for herself what she wants and welcomes Justin into her life instead.

Soon, Abbie spends less time in the doll shop and more nights with him as she grows apart from her cousin and the routine of her usual life. Laura becomes possessive, desperate, and selfish as she tries to keep her influence over Abbie. Laura won’t share the spotlight, and when expected to, she works to destroy everything that Abbie loves. To push back, Abbie further unravels Laura’s control, string by string. She stops making dolls, and she breaks ones that already exist, and to break a doll is to take away its power.

Abbie never wanted to lose her best friend, but she confronts the reality that Laura might not be the person she thought she knew. She must choose between Laura or the new life she’s creating without her before her world is damaged beyond repair.

My name is Randi Muilenburg, and I currently live in Colorado with my math-enthusiast boyfriend, our eight-month-old puppy named Cricket, and more cases of Dr. Pepper than I have room for. I graduated from Metropolitan State University of Denver in December 2016 with a BA in English and Journalism.
